About us

The monitoring and controlling phase happens simultaneously with the execution phase. It involves tracking the project’s progress to ensure it stays on schedule and within budget. Key performance indicators (KPIs) are used to measure how well the project is performing. If any deviations from the plan are identified, corrective actions are taken to bring the project back on track. Changes in scope, budget, or timeline are managed through a change control process to avoid disruptions. Risks are continuously monitored, and any issues that arise are resolved promptly. Regular status reports are shared with stakeholders to keep them informed. This phase ensures that the project remains under control and meets its objectives. The closure phase marks the end of the project. All deliverables are completed, and the final product or service is handed over to the client or sponsor.
